III. DETAILED COUNTRY PROFILES

1. PORTUGAL

Visa Pathways

  • D7 "Passive Income" Visa: ‚Ç¨870/month minimum income, renewable 2 years
  • D8 Digital Nomad Visa: ‚Ç¨3,480/month income from foreign sources, 1-year renewable
  • D2 Entrepreneur Visa: ‚Ç¨10,440 living expenses + business plan
  • Start-up Visa: Requires approved incubator acceptance

Financial Requirements

  • D7 Family of Four: ‚Ç¨1,827/month (‚Ç¨870 main + ‚Ç¨435 spouse + ‚Ç¨261 per child)
  • D8 Family of Four: ‚Ç¨18,792 total savings required
  • Proof of accommodation (rental lease) required
  • Private health insurance mandatory initially
Recent Policy Updates (2024-2025):
  • NHR Tax Regime Closed: No new applicants after March 2024
  • Golden Visa real estate route eliminated (2023)
  • SEF restructured to AIMA with processing improvements
  • D7/D8 visas remain unchanged and welcomed

Education Options

  • Public schools: Free, Portuguese language
  • International schools: ‚Ç¨2,000-‚Ç¨20,000/year
  • Strong integration support for foreign students
  • Growing bilingual program availability

Tax Implications

  • Post-NHR: Full Portuguese taxation on worldwide income (14.5-48%)
  • Israel-Portugal tax treaty prevents double taxation
  • Remote work from Israeli companies taxed in Portugal
  • No wealth tax, moderate capital gains treatment

Permanent Residency & Citizenship

  • Citizenship after 5 years (shortened from 6 in 2022)
  • A2 Portuguese language test required
  • Dual citizenship allowed (keep Israeli passport)
  • Minimum 16 months residence in first 2 years required

Lifestyle & Integration

  • Safety: Top 5 globally in Global Peace Index
  • Climate: Mediterranean, mild winters
  • Healthcare: Public SNS system, initially need private insurance
  • Community: Growing expat population (~7% foreign-born)
  • Cost: Lower than most Western Europe, rising in Lisbon
Best For: Easy entry, fast citizenship, dual nationality, mild climate

2. SPAIN

Visa Pathways

  • Digital Nomad Visa: ‚Ç¨2,368/month minimum (200% SMI), extendable to 5 years
  • Non-Lucrative Visa: ‚Ç¨2,400/month, no work allowed (including remote)
  • Entrepreneur Visa: Business plan approval, innovative project required
  • Golden Visa: Property route abolished April 2025

Financial Requirements

  • Digital Nomad Family of Four: ‚Ç¨46,176/year total
  • Additional 75% SMI for spouse (‚Ç¨888), 25% per child (‚Ç¨296)
  • Private health insurance (no co-pay) required
  • Extensive apostilled documentation needed
Recent Policy Updates (2024-2025):
  • Startup Law (2023): Created Digital Nomad visa, tax benefits
  • Golden Visa Abolished: No more ‚Ç¨500,000 property investments
  • 20% income cap from Spanish clients for nomads
  • Enhanced online application processes

Education Options

  • Public schools: Free, Spanish language (regional variations)
  • International schools: ‚Ç¨5,000-‚Ç¨20,000/year
  • 281 international schools nationwide
  • Strong bilingual programs in Madrid region

Tax Implications

  • Beckham Law: 24% flat rate on Spanish income for 6 years
  • Foreign income exempt under Beckham regime
  • Standard residents: 19-47% progressive rates
  • Startup incentives: 15% corporate rate first 4 years

Permanent Residency & Citizenship

  • Citizenship after 10 years (longest requirement)
  • DELE A2 Spanish + CCSE cultural test required
  • Dual citizenship restrictions: Must renounce Israeli citizenship
  • Permanent residency after 5 years available

Lifestyle & Integration

  • Healthcare: Excellent public system, private insurance initially
  • Safety: Very low violent crime
  • Community: Large expat populations, smaller Israeli community
  • Climate: Varies by region, generally Mediterranean
  • Integration: Spanish language essential for full integration
Best For: Tax benefits (first 6 years), excellent lifestyle, strong startup ecosystem

3. IRELAND

Visa Pathways

  • Stamp 0 (Independent Means): ‚Ç¨50,000/year per person, non-working
  • STEP (Start-up Entrepreneur): ‚Ç¨50,000 business funding, 2-year initial permit
  • Critical Skills Employment: Tech sector opportunities
  • Investor Programme: Closed February 2023

Financial Requirements

  • Stamp 0: ‚Ç¨100,000/year for couple + additional for children
  • STEP: ‚Ç¨50,000 startup funding + living expenses proof
  • Detailed accountant's financial verification required
  • Private medical insurance mandatory
Recent Policy Updates (2024-2025):
  • IIP (Golden Visa) closed permanently
  • STEP program streamlined with online applications
  • Discussions ongoing for dedicated remote work visa
  • Enhanced tech sector promotion post-Brexit

Education Options

  • Primary advantage: English-language instruction
  • Public schools: Free, high quality, often religious affiliation
  • International schools: ‚Ç¨4,000-‚Ç¨10,000/year (much lower than elsewhere)
  • No language barrier for integration

Tax Implications

  • Non-domiciled regime: Foreign income taxed only if remitted to Ireland
  • Progressive rates: 20% up to ‚Ç¨40,000, 40% above
  • 12.5% corporate tax for trading companies
  • Israel-Ireland tax treaty (2019) prevents double taxation

Permanent Residency & Citizenship

  • Citizenship after 5 years - among EU's fastest
  • No language test required (English sufficient)
  • Dual citizenship fully allowed
  • Continuous residence requirement strictly enforced

Lifestyle & Integration

  • Language: English primary language, enormous advantage
  • Community: Small but present Israeli community
  • Safety: Extremely safe, low crime rates
  • Healthcare: Two-tier system, private insurance recommended
  • Climate: Cool, wet, limited sunshine
Best For: English speakers, fast citizenship, dual nationality, family-friendly

4. NETHERLANDS

Visa Pathways

  • Start-up Visa: 1-year with approved facilitator, transitions to self-employment
  • Self-Employment Permit: Points-based system (90/300 minimum)
  • Highly Skilled Migrant: ‚Ç¨5,000/month salary threshold
  • No friendship treaty with Israel (unlike US nationals)

Financial Requirements

  • Startup visa: ‚Ç¨16,800/year minimum (70% minimum wage)
  • Family of four: ~‚Ç¨30,000-40,000 recommended
  • Facilitator agreement with approved Dutch incubator required
  • Detailed business plan evaluation by RVO
Recent Policy Updates (2024-2025):
  • Citizenship changes proposed: 10-year requirement (up from 5)
  • B1 Dutch language requirement (up from A2)
  • Dual citizenship restrictions: May be enforced more strictly
  • Startup ecosystem continues expansion

Education Options

  • Public schools: Free, Dutch language with integration support
  • Dutch International Schools: ‚Ç¨3,600-‚Ç¨8,000/year (subsidized)
  • Private international: ‚Ç¨15,000-‚Ç¨25,000/year
  • Excellent bilingual program options

Tax Implications

  • 30% Ruling: Tax-free 30% of salary for 5 years (if eligible)
  • Progressive rates: 37% up to ‚Ç¨73,000, 49.5% above
  • 19% corporate tax (first ‚Ç¨200,000)
  • Complex Box 1/2/3 system for different income types

Permanent Residency & Citizenship

  • Current: 5 years to citizenship
  • Proposed: 10 years (significant change)
  • Dual citizenship: Currently restricted, may become stricter
  • B1 Dutch language requirement

Lifestyle & Integration

  • Quality of life: Consistently top-rated
  • Cycling culture: Excellent infrastructure
  • Community: Strong Israeli tech community (~3,000-5,000)
  • English proficiency: Very high, especially in cities
  • Healthcare: Mandatory insurance system, high quality
Best For: Tech entrepreneurs, startup ecosystem, quality of life (if dual citizenship not priority)

5. GERMANY

Visa Pathways

  • Freelance/Self-Employment Visa: Viable business plan required
  • Startup Visa: Innovative tech founders, no minimum investment
  • EU Blue Card: ‚Ç¨45,300 minimum for IT professionals
  • Financially Independent: Rare, substantial passive income needed

Financial Requirements

  • Freelance: ‚Ç¨2,000-3,000/month income demonstration
  • Startup: ‚Ç¨25,000-30,000 recommended for family
  • Processing: 4-12 weeks
  • Comprehensive health insurance required
Recent Policy Updates (2024-2025):
  • Skilled Immigration Act 2.0: Lowered Blue Card thresholds
  • Startup visa expansion: More approved incubators
  • Dual citizenship now allowed: Major change from previous renunciation requirement
  • "Opportunity Card" for job seekers introduced

Education Options

  • Public schools: Free, German language
  • International schools: ‚Ç¨12,000-25,000/year
  • Strong bilingual programs in major cities
  • Integration support for foreign children

Tax Implications

  • Progressive: 14-45% on worldwide income
  • Corporate: ~30% total (15% + trade tax)
  • Israel-Germany tax treaty applies
  • R&D incentives for startups

Permanent Residency & Citizenship

  • Citizenship after 5-8 years (5 with integration course)
  • Dual citizenship allowed (major 2024 change)
  • B1 German language requirement
  • Blue Card holders: PR after 21 months with B1 German

Lifestyle & Integration

  • Healthcare: Excellent public system
  • Safety: Very high, family-friendly
  • Community: Large Israeli community (15,000+ in Berlin)
  • Startup hubs: Berlin, Munich strong ecosystems
  • Language: English common in tech/business
Best For: Large Israeli community, dual citizenship allowed, strong economy

6. UNITED KINGDOM

Visa Pathways

  • Innovator Founder Visa: Replaced startup visa 2023, needs endorsement
  • Skilled Worker Visa: ¬£38,700 minimum salary
  • Global Talent Visa: Recognized leaders in tech
  • High Potential Individual: Top university graduates

Financial Requirements

  • Innovator Founder: ¬£1,270/month for family of four minimum
  • Must obtain endorsing body approval
  • Processing: 3-8 weeks
  • Health surcharge: ¬£1,035/year
Recent Policy Updates (2024-2025):
  • Salary thresholds raised significantly
  • Investor visa abolished (2022)
  • Non-dom regime being phased out (April 2025 changes)
  • Stricter entrepreneur tests implemented

Education Options

  • Major advantage: English language instruction
  • State schools: Free, high quality
  • International schools: ¬£15,000-40,000/year
  • No language barrier for children

Tax Implications

  • Income tax: 20-45%
  • Non-dom regime ending: Will affect foreign income taxation
  • Corporate: 19% (25% for profits >¬£250,000)
  • Capital gains: 10-20%

Permanent Residency & Citizenship

  • ILR after 5 years, citizenship after 6 total
  • Life in UK test + English B1 required
  • Dual citizenship allowed
  • Children born to ILR holders get citizenship

Lifestyle & Integration

  • NHS: Free healthcare (after surcharge payment)
  • Community: Large Israeli community in London
  • Cost: High, especially London
  • Culture: No language barrier, familiar systems
Best For: English speakers, no language barrier, established systems

7. CANADA

Visa Pathways

  • Start-up Visa Program: Designated organization support required
  • Express Entry: Points-based skilled worker system
  • Provincial Nominee Programs: Province-specific streams
  • Self-employed Persons: Relevant experience required

Financial Requirements

  • Startup Visa: CAD $13,757/person proof of funds
  • Express Entry: ~470+ CRS points needed
  • Processing: 6-18 months
  • Family included in all applications
Recent Policy Updates (2024-2025):
  • Category-based draws: Tech workers prioritized
  • French speakers: Bonus points awarded
  • Startup processing: Accelerated to 12-18 months
  • Provincial digital nomad programs emerging

Education Options

  • Public schools: Free, excellent quality
  • French immersion programs available
  • International schools: CAD $15,000-35,000/year
  • No language barrier in English provinces

Tax Implications

  • Combined federal/provincial: 20-53%
  • Small business rate: 9% federal + provincial
  • Generous R&D tax credits
  • Israel-Canada tax treaty applies

Permanent Residency & Citizenship

  • PR immediate with most programs
  • Citizenship after 3 years as PR (1,095 days in 5 years)
  • Dual citizenship allowed
  • Very welcoming policies

Lifestyle & Integration

  • Healthcare: Universal system
  • Safety: Extremely high
  • Community: Large Israeli communities (Toronto, Montreal)
  • Climate: Cold winters except Vancouver
  • Quality of life: Consistently top-rated
Best For: Fastest to citizenship, family-friendly, tech opportunities

8. UNITED STATES

Visa Pathways

  • E-2 Treaty Investor: Israelis eligible, substantial business investment
  • L-1A: Intra-company transfer for US branch
  • O-1: Extraordinary ability in business/tech
  • EB-5: Investment immigration ($800,000-$1,050,000)

Financial Requirements

  • E-2: Typically $100,000+ investment, active business required
  • No direct green card path from E-2
  • Processing: 2-6 months (premium available)
  • Spouses can work, children can study
Recent Policy Updates (2024-2025):
  • H-1B lottery changes: Reduced gaming
  • O-1 criteria loosened: Better for tech professionals
  • EB-5 amounts increased: Higher investment thresholds
  • Global Entry: Israel added to program

Education Options

  • Public schools: Free, quality varies by district
  • International schools: $20,000-50,000/year
  • Universities: Excellent but expensive
  • No language barrier

Tax Implications

  • Federal: 10-37% + state taxes (0-13%)
  • Complex international rules: GILTI, PFIC
  • Corporate: 21% federal + state
  • Exit tax: If giving up green card after 8+ years

Permanent Residency & Citizenship

  • Green card timelines: Vary significantly by visa type
  • Citizenship after 5 years as green card holder
  • Dual citizenship allowed (though not encouraged)
  • Children born in US are citizens

Lifestyle & Integration

  • Healthcare: Expensive, private insurance needed
  • Community: Large Israeli communities (NYC, LA, Miami)
  • Mobility: Car-dependent in most areas
  • Salaries: High in tech hubs
  • Diversity: Varies greatly by location
Best For: High earners, tech entrepreneurs, established Israeli communities
